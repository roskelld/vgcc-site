Game Seers is a video game project validation service based in the UK and Canada.

Work with us

Approach

Here's our approach to help people get an edge on video game investment proposals.

Great video games take you into fantasy worlds where anything is possible, but building them isn't magic. The steps to designing and developing a game is grounded in to a process of knowing the limitations of technology, picking the right challenges to face, being open minded to critic and ready to be challenged.

Our goal is take a game pitch in whatever form it currently exists and step it through a set of tests to perform a risk assessment. Providing you with a report to help improve your assessment and empower you with a better set of questions to explore.

Formula

We reach our destination by following a simple formula

Plain & Simple Language

Whether it's an email, text in a report or video chat, we aim to speak in plain, jargon free language. It's our goal to demystify and help better understand.

Read Between the Lines

We apply our own learnings to a project to look for questions that we'd want to see answered. We also look for gaps in the information from which new challenges might emerge.

Provide an Actionable Reponse

The goal of our feedback and reporting is to empower the reader with valuable action items, questions or topics to investigate or to understand where further work may be.

Learn

learn about how we've spent decades experiencing first hand what it takes to create great —and not so great— games.

If there's a short way to sell our value then it's: We've spent a combined near 40 year period designing, developing and releasing video games; we've seen the pitfalls, experienced the challenges, suffered the lows, and celebrated the highs of it all.

Whilst we're not done yet in our journey to create games on current and future platforms, we found ourselves thinking about ways in which we can help others with the knowledge and experiences we have.

Our conclusion was to set up a service at the intersection of developers and funding. Games need funding to be made, but how do investors differentiate between great and not so great ideas? We believe that we can offer help there by looking at those ideas from our perspective as developers, presenting our thoughts in plain and bullsh*t free language.

Whether you're a small investor or part of a large group, our approach is to work with you in a respectable and valued way, by listening to your process and communicating our findings crisply and clearly.
